The Challenge

The Problem Space

Healthcare AI startups are racing to build foundation models that could revolutionize drug discovery, medical imaging, and clinical care. But they're hitting a wall. Training a protein folding model requires thousands of GPU hours. Analyzing genomic datasets at scale needs massive parallel compute. And healthcare AI can't afford to fail: models must be accurate, explainable, and clinically validated. Most startups can't afford the compute infrastructure they need but also don't know where they should start. Cloud credits run out etc. They're forced to choose between model performance and runway.

My Role

As a Director at the Creative Destruction Lab, I scaled the North Amercian and European life sciences and therapeutics-focused innovation ecosystem by:

  • Identifying and recruiting high-potential health startups across drug discovery, medical imaging, genomics, and clinical AI
  • Conducted hundreds of founder interviews to assess technical novelty and market potential
  • Assessing technical readiness and matching startups to the right cloud infrastructure provider based on partnerships with CDL. Facilitating strategic partnerships between startups and healthcare enterprises, research institutions, and VCs.
  • Launched CDL Cancer in partnership with the Canadian Cancer Society, recruiting cancer tech startups across diagnostics and therapeutics from the UK (Oxford), Canada, and the US. I built the entire ecosystem: recruited the founding team, sourced founders, aligned VCs, brought in technical experts and academic advisors, and created the infrastructure to systematically accelerate cancer innovation.
