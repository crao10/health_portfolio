Drug Discovery

Protein Engineering from Nobel Prize-Winning Science

The Challenge: A-Alpha Bio, a spinout from Nobel Prize winner David Baker's lab at the University of Washington's Institute for Protein Design, developed AlphaSeq—a revolutionary platform for measuring protein-protein interactions at unprecedented scale. The technology uses genetically engineered yeast cells and next-generation sequencing to characterize millions of protein binding interactions simultaneously, solving a critical bottleneck in antibody and drug discovery. The startup had groundbreaking science but faced the classic commercialization challenge: translating a sophisticated yeast display platform into partnerships with pharmaceutical companies who needed to understand why this approach was superior to existing methods.

My Work:

  • • Helped articulate the platform's competitive advantages: 100x more protein interaction data than microfluidic platforms, ability to optimize drug candidates against multiple pathogen variants simultaneously.
  • • Wrote detailed investor memos translating the technical innovation (library-on-library screening, quantitative binding affinity data via yeast mating) into clear pharmaceutical value propositions.
  • • Facilitated connections to pharmaceutical partners and investors who could validate the approach for high-impact disease targets including cancer and infectious diseases

Climate & Life Sciences

Cultivated Protein with Functional Fat Integration

The Challenge: A biotech startup developed a breakthrough method for incorporating functional adipose tissue into cultivated protein—solving one of the cellular agriculture industry's hardest technical problems: replicating the marbling and mouthfeel of conventional meat. While the science was validated in small-scale bioreactors, they faced the classic commercialization valley of death: unclear regulatory pathway, capital-intensive scale-up requirements, and a market skeptical of "lab-grown meat" economics.

My Work:

  • • Translated technical innovation to commercial narrative: Interviewed the founder (a talented postdoc with deep expertise in cellular differentiation) and leveraged my biochemistry background to translate the breakthrough (functional adipose tissue integration) into a compelling commercial narrative that emphasized the competitive advantages.
  • • Technical validation with domain experts: Recruited cellular agriculture researchers and food scientists to assess the legitimacy of the technical innovation (differentiation protocols and lipid integration mechanisms) and surface any risks that needed to be addressed.
  • • GTM strategy: Worked with cultivated meat industry veterans and regulatory consultants to map the unique commercialization challenges—multi-year FDA approval timelines, capital requirements for GMP manufacturing facilities, and consumer acceptance hurdles.
